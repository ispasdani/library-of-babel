The Library of Babel

after the story by Jorge Luis Borges · 1941

The universe — which others call the Library — is an indefinite number of hexagonal galleries. Each holds 640 volumes; each volume, 410 pages; each page, forty lines of eighty characters drawn from twenty-five symbols. Every book that can exist, exists — somewhere — exactly once per cycle.

This replica is total: every shelf is real, every page is permanent, and any text you can write already waits in a fixed hexagon. You may ask the Library to take you to it.

Walk: WASD · Look: mouse · Interact: E or click · Seek: T · Help: H
On touch screens: left thumb to walk, right thumb to look, tap prompts to interact.

On the use of the Library

Walking. WASD or arrow keys; the mouse turns your gaze (click the world to capture it, Esc to release). Each gallery's two free sides open onto hallways that lead to the next, identical gallery. In every hallway: two closets (one for sleeping upright, one for other necessities), a mirror, and the spiral stairway that sinks and soars to other floors.

Reading. Aim at any volume and press E or click to draw it from the shelf. Its 410 pages are not invented as you watch — they are computed from the book's fixed address, the same for every visitor, forever.

Seeking. Press T and write anything. The Library will name a hexagon — its floor and gallery numbers usually run to thousands of digits — where that text stands on a page, and will carry you there. The volume will be lit; the passage, marked.

The shaft. The railing around the air shaft is very low. Approach it and press E if you must.

Alphabet: 22 letters plus the space, the comma, and the period — 25 symbols. Your j, k, w and x are transliterated (i, c, v, s), as the Library spells in older tongues. Numerals and capitals do not exist here.

Seek a text

Write up to one page (3,200 symbols). Somewhere, a volume already contains it; the Library will compute one of its innumerable locations.

The spiral stairway

It sinks abysmally and soars upward to remote distances. The floors are without number.

The railing

It is very low. Beyond it the shaft falls past floor after lit, identical floor, without visible end. The dead of the Library are given to this air.
